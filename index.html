About Myself

I was born in Pasadena to my loving Parents Mark and Dara Fleming. My mother shared with me her love of cooking and the arts of cooking and being open and generous to others. My dad shared with me his love of athletics and humility it takes to provide for those your love.

I played every sport under the sun growing up and fell in love with writing. For college I attended UC Santa Barbra where I studied Religious Studies and focused my research on State Formation in Iran and Tibetan Buddhism. Later in college I gravitated athletically towards rock climbing and have found so many wonderful people and experiences through climbing.

Upon graduation wanted to help frontline-communities affected by wildfires in my home state of California. I was hired on as an Environmental Scientist for Jesco on the Campfire Cleanup Project. As an Environmental Unit Leader, I worked in the field testing for contaminants in the ruins of residents homes and testing the soil after those structures had been cleared.

I am extremely grateful to have been a part this project. While participating in the cleanup project I also saw the need and potential for programming in the field of Environmental Science. I decided to learn programming in the hopes to help develop technology to help the human community

Paradise Murals

I saw Shane Grammer's work every day in Paradise. His incredible art was a constant and potent reminder of the deep human element omnipresent within my work as a scientist

As I spent thousands of hours executing protocal and metholdogies so "Hot Zones" could become Homes again, listening to community members stories allowed me a deeper connection to the purpose of my work. These murals became a static visual representation of those stories, one that wasn't created by disaster but by humans.
